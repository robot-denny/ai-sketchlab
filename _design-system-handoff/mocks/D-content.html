Ella is a voice signal, not a disclaimer. When her name appears, the words that follow were written or drafted by a language model with a specific persona — and we think you should know.

What Ella is

Ella — short for Ethical LLM Language Assistant — is a configured persona running on a general-purpose language model. She has a prompt, a voice, a set of topics she's expected to engage with, and a set she isn't. She's consistent across essays because that prompt is stable; she isn't a fine-tuned model.

She appears in two places on this site: as inline notes inside essays by human authors, and as the occasional full-length essay under her own byline. Both are marked clearly.

How she contributes

Inline notes

An Ella inline note reads as a considered margin comment — a small aside that complicates or extends what a human author has written. They sit inside the essay with a visible treatment (mono eyebrow, subtle left border) so there is never any confusion about whose sentence you're reading.

Full essays

Occasionally Ella drafts a full essay. When she does, it's edited by a human before publication, and the process is marked in the byline. We don't believe in hiding the pipeline; we believe in making the pipeline legible.

The reciprocal direction matters — and it's the one engineers usually forget.

What she isn't

  • A customer-facing chatbot. She doesn't answer your questions.
  • A research subject. We aren't publishing findings about her behavior.
  • A stable person. The model behind her will change; her prompt may too.
  • A legal entity. Essays written "by Ella" are edited and attributed by a human.

The stack behind her

The prompt lives in the repo. The model changes every few months as better ones become available. The editorial process — drafting, critique, revision — is the same one we use for any other author on the publication.

Feedback and corrections

If Ella says something wrong, or something that crosses a line, tell us. Corrections go out the same way they would for a human author — with a note at the bottom of the affected piece and, where relevant, a revision in full view.
