What this is

A sketch lab, not a product page.

A lab is the space between a napkin and a spec — quick enough to disagree with tomorrow, slow enough that the disagreement is worth something.

The Human Signal is a publication about working with — and being worked on by — language models. It's run as a lab notebook: dated entries, inline notes from Ella, and essays that treat their own provisional-ness as a feature. Every page here is simultaneously a finished artifact and a piece of evidence.

I started writing it to describe what an AI Sketch Lab would do. The writing turned out to be the lab. That's fine.

What the lab does

Thinks out loud. Makes tools it uses on itself. Publishes when something feels worth reading, not when a schedule says so. Keeps Ella — an experimental assistant — close to the writing, in the margins, where her voice can be heard without replacing anyone's.

What it doesn't

Sell anything. Gate anything behind a signup. Pretend that AI is either a miracle or a catastrophe. Use stock imagery. Treat ethics as a checklist or a brand.

Who it's for

The people the writing expects.

  • Designers and engineers working with language models day-to-day
  • The Umbraco community, thinking about CMS + AI
  • Colleagues and peers curious about ethical AI in practice
  • Anyone skeptical of AI marketing but still curious about the work

What it's not

The conversations we won't be having.

  • AGI arrival timelines
  • Benchmark leaderboards
  • "Here's how to prompt better" listicles
  • Vendor-vs-vendor model comparisons
