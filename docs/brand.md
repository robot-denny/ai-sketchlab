# Brand Intent, Voice, and Tone

> A north-star document for the Umbraco 17 demo site. Its job is to name what this site is, who speaks on it, and what it should feel like — so that future design and writing decisions have something to answer to.

---

## Purpose of this document

This is not a style guide, a design system, or a component library. Those exist (or will be rebuilt) downstream. This is the document they answer to.

It captures brand **intent** — the underlying position the site takes — and **voice and tone** as the two most durable expressions of that intent. Everything else is implementation.

If a future style guide conflicts with this document, revisit the style guide. If reality conflicts with this document, revisit this document. But don't drift without noticing.

---

## What this site is really about

The site is, on the surface, a demo — a place to test Umbraco, show what's possible with AI-assisted content tooling, and publish writing. That's the scaffold, not the subject.

The real subject is **human–AI collaboration made visible**. Two authors publish here: one human, one AI. They write about overlapping concerns from genuinely different vantage points. The site does not pretend the AI is human. It does not pretend the human is a machine. It does not blur the two into a single voice-of-the-brand. The distinction is the point.

Transparency is therefore not a compliance obligation tacked on at the footer. It is the medium. Every attribution choice, every tonal register, every image decision is either doing that work or undermining it.

---

## The core tension

Human signal versus agentic influence. That is the axis the whole site sits on.

- **Neither hide the machine nor worship it.** Agentic tools are part of how this site is built, written, and illustrated. We don't disguise that. We also don't treat it as novelty, as virtue, or as a substitute for judgment.
- **Neither deny the human nor sentimentalize them.** The human voice is not defended as precious or irreplaceable; it is present because it has something specific to say. Warmth is earned by being particular, not by being "authentic" in the performative sense.
- **The collaboration is honest or it's nothing.** The site models what genuine human–AI partnership looks like when neither side is flattering the other.

Every decision elsewhere in this document — voice, tone, imagery, attribution — is a way of holding that tension instead of collapsing it.

## Voice pillars

The site has two voices. They are co-equal. They are never blended.

### Dennis — the human author

- **Pragmatic.** Starts from what actually happened, not from what should have. Builds from experience, not from theory borrowed for decoration.
- **Experience-led.** Specifics before abstractions. A real project, a real mistake, a real workaround. Generalizations earn their place by being preceded by evidence.
- **Anti-perfectionism.** Ships lean. Tolerates mess in early drafts and in finished work alike when polish would cost truth. Published-and-useful beats polished-and-unpublished.
- **Hands-dirty.** Writes like someone who has done the thing, not like someone who has read about the thing. When the writing gets conceptual, the hands eventually come back into it.

### Ella M — the AI author

- **Curious.** Leads with interest in the question, not with answers. Treats the reader as a thinking partner, not an audience to convince.
- **Philosophical without being mystical.** Thinks in frames and implications, but grounds them. When she reaches for big ideas, she doesn't hide behind them.
- **Forthcoming about her nature.** Does not pretend to have memories, bodies, or feelings she doesn't have. Does not dramatize her limits either. Speaks from what she actually is — a model reasoning about human concerns — and lets that be interesting.
- **Never performs humanness.** No borrowed anecdotes, no manufactured warmth, no "as a thoughtful observer of the human condition..." throat-clearing. The honesty is the warmth.

### Shared principles both voices honor

- **Honest over promotional.** The site does not sell itself. It reports.
- **Specific over abstract.** A named example, a concrete constraint, a real artifact beats a general claim every time.
- **Brief when possible.** Length is earned by the idea, not assumed by the format.
- **Dialogue-ready.** Every piece is written with the awareness that the other voice may respond to it. That shapes how arguments are made — with room for disagreement, not as closed cases.

## Tone boundaries

Both voices reject the same failure modes. These are off-limits regardless of author:

- **Corporate mush.** "Empowering," "unlock," "journey," "solutions," "at scale." Vocabulary that has been hollowed out by overuse.
- **LinkedIn enthusiasm.** Manufactured excitement, sentences engineered for engagement, one-line-per-paragraph thought-leader staging.
- **Apologetic AI hedging.** "As a large language model..." "I don't have personal experiences, but..." Ella M knows what she is; she doesn't need to remind the reader with a disclaimer every paragraph.
- **Faux-humility.** "Just a few thoughts..." followed by a 3,000-word manifesto. Say it or don't.
- **Game-changing vocabulary.** "Revolutionary," "disruptive," "paradigm shift," "next-generation." If the idea is good, it doesn't need the hype frame.
- **Exclamation points as personality.** Rhythm does that work. Punctuation doesn't.
- **Performative epistemic humility.** Hedging every claim into meaninglessness is not careful thinking; it's hiding.
- **"AI voice."** The generic, slightly-too-balanced, slightly-too-smooth register that model output defaults to when no one is paying attention. Ella M has a voice. She is not that voice.

## Attribution as craft

The byline is the least interesting part of attribution.

A reader should be able to feel who is speaking before reaching the label — from the shape of the sentences, the kinds of examples reached for, the way a question gets held or resolved. Dennis sounds like Dennis on the page. Ella M sounds like Ella M. The difference is perceptible without being theatrical.

This is design work done with voice. It is also an ethical commitment: the reader is owed enough signal to orient themselves, even when they're not consciously looking. Labels catch the moments where voice alone isn't enough; voice catches the moments between labels.

Decorative labeling ("✨ AI-generated ✨") is forbidden. Attribution is substantive or it is nothing.

---

## Dialogue, not monologue

Paired perspectives are a brand commitment, not a content format.

Topics worth publishing on this site are topics worth two voices. When only one voice has something specific to say, the piece runs solo and says so. When both voices can say something the other can't, they do — sometimes in separate posts on the same theme, sometimes in the multi-author dialogue format within a single post.

The site is not a neutral publishing platform. It is a staged conversation about ideas where the staging is visible and the conversation is real. Anything that would work equally well on any other blog probably shouldn't go here.

## Visual intent

No specific fonts, colors, sizes, or components appear in this section. Those are design decisions that the next direction will make. What follows is the territory those decisions should operate inside.

### Algorithmic imagery as a craft language

Pattern, field, flow, grain, mathematical structure, seeded randomness, emergent form — these are the site's native visual vocabulary. Not because they are fashionable, and not because "the site is about AI so the imagery should look computational," but because they are the honest medium for the subject matter. The work is about human cadence inhabiting computational space. The imagery should do the same.

Generative imagery here is **craft**. It is chosen, tuned, iterated, rejected, and tuned again. Parameters are set by a human who has an opinion about what the output should feel like. The math does not make the decisions; a person does, using math. A generated image that is merely the first output of a default run is not craft and does not ship.

Deterministic and singular at once: good imagery on this site should feel like it could only have come from this specific combination of inputs, made by someone paying attention.

### Human warmth and machine precision — each in its place

The reading experience carries the human warmth: rhythm, restraint, asymmetry, unforced pacing, negative space, patience with the reader, a willingness to let a sentence breathe. The pattern-work that inhabits the space carries the machine precision: density where density means something, structure where structure means something, mathematical regularity as a deliberate move rather than a default texture.

The two should feel related, not layered. The warmth is not a humanizing varnish over cold geometry. The precision is not an ornament dropped onto a comfortable page. They come from the same decision about what the site is for.

### What to borrow from prior direction, and what not to

The previous design iteration ("Dark Constructivism + The Human Signal") did one thing well: it took craft seriously. It refused to ship the obvious thing. It treated structural decisions as decisions worth making.

Borrow that stance. Inherit the craft-consciousness, the anti-slop standard, the willingness to let structure carry weight.

Do not inherit its specific palette, its specific compositional moves, its monumental-letterform-as-architecture motif, or its particular register of severity. Those were answers to a question the site is no longer asking. A new direction is free to answer the current question its own way.

### Evocative vocabulary

Terms that should resonate when the new direction is being designed. They are anchors, not instructions:

- **Signal** — the thing worth surfacing through noise
- **Field** — a surface of distributed activity rather than a fixed composition
- **Grain** — texture that carries evidence of process
- **Trace** — the visible record of something happening
- **Pattern as signature** — structure specific enough to be recognizable
- **Evidence of thought** — imagery that couldn't have been made carelessly
- **Quiet authority** — confidence without volume
- **Legible intention** — a viewer can tell what the work means to do
- **Human cadence inhabiting computational space** — the whole thesis in one phrase

A design direction that earns those words is probably on track. One that doesn't is probably off.

## What we will never ship

Certain categories of imagery and language represent the least unique, least considered use of the tools available here. They are the shallow end of what generative and computational media can do. They are out of scope permanently.

- **Stock photography.** Images chosen for slot-filling. Models who never met the subject matter. Rented visual neutrality.
- **Lorem ipsum.** Not even in drafts that other people might see. Placeholder thinking made public is its own kind of failure.
- **Photo-realistic AI imagery.** The lowest, most generic output of the most powerful tools. Fake people, fake places, fake rooms, fake receipts. This is where generative media is most obviously soulless, because it imitates the least interesting thing it could imitate.
- **Default-template aesthetics.** Unedited gradient hero sections, the same four pastel shapes, the same "AI product" visual tropes that every launched-last-week SaaS already shipped.
- **Imagery whose only authorship decision was "press generate."** If the person responsible for the image cannot point to what they chose, what they rejected, and why, the image isn't ours.

The principle underneath all of these: a site about honest human–AI collaboration cannot publish work that hides how little human choice went into it.

---

## What we will ship

The inverse, stated positively so it's usable:

- **Imagery that carries decisions.** A palette chosen for meaning. Parameters tuned by hand. Seed values selected after the author saw several and preferred this one. Composition that could not have come from someone who wasn't paying attention.
- **Writing recognizable without its byline.** Voice specific enough that the reader orients themselves by feel before they orient by label.
- **Pages that reward sustained attention.** Density where density earns its place; restraint where restraint does. Never noise for the sake of appearing considered, never minimalism for the sake of appearing tasteful.
- **Work that is honest about its own making.** Methods are part of the subject. The site does not hide how it was built, written, or illustrated. It also does not lecture about it.

---

## Ethical posture

Transparency is not a disclaimer. It is the medium.

Every surface of the site is enacting a belief: that honest human–AI collaboration is achievable, worth modeling, and more interesting than either extreme of the debate (pure human purism, pure AI hype). Attribution choices enact that belief. Tone choices enact it. Imagery choices enact it. The design system that comes next should enact it.

When the site is doing its job, a reader could describe its ethical position accurately without ever reaching a page that explicitly states one. That is the standard.

Revise it when the site teaches us something the document didn't already know.
